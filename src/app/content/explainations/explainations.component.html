Moiré Patterns and Twisted Bilayer Graphene

This app interactively introduces the principle of Moiré patterns.

According to Wikipedia: " [...] moiré patterns [...] are large-scale interference patterns that can be produced when an opaque ruled pattern with transparent gaps is overlaid on another similar pattern." In the here shown case the Moiré pattern is produced by overlaying two hexagonal lattice-structures with one another twisting the upper one by a chosen angle - try it out!

Relation to Physics

The Moiré pattern on the left resembles that of twisted bilayer graphene. This is a material that is formed by placing two hexagonal lattices of Carbon atoms (so called Graphene) ontop of each other and twisting them.

In 2018 the group of Pablo Jarillo-Herrero at MIT found fascinating physical properties of this material - espacially at a so called magic angle of 1.05° - type it in and look at the pattern! They published their results in the journal nature - check it out!.

By now there exist many more examples of materials that realize a Moiré pattern. Their properties are subject to intense research under the topic of Twistronics.
